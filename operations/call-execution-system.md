# Call Execution System

## Purpose

Every Contractor Circle call should create implementation, retention, proof, and content.

Every call should do five jobs:

```text
1. Teach one principle.
2. Install one tool or behavior.
3. Solve one real member problem.
4. Create reusable content.
5. Create retention by making members feel progress.
```

If a call does not do those five things, it is too expensive emotionally.

## Standard Call Format

```text
Contractor Circle Implementation Call

1. Opening frame - 5 minutes
2. This week's leak - 10 minutes
3. Tool/process training - 20 minutes
4. Member hot seat / teardown - 30 minutes
5. Implementation assignment - 15 minutes
6. Wins, stuck points, next actions - 10 minutes
7. Close with Circle roadmap - 5 minutes
```

If the call is longer, expand the hot seat and Q&A.

Do not expand the rambling.

## Example Call: Change-Order Cash

Opening frame:

```text
Today we are fixing one leak: extra work that does not become approved, billed, and collected cash.
```

Principle:

```text
A change order is not a paperwork issue. It is a cash-control issue.
```

Tool:

- Change Order Cash Tracker.

Member teardown questions:

```text
What changed?
Who requested it?
When did it happen?
Was it priced?
Was it submitted?
Was it approved?
Was it billed?
Was it collected?
Who owns the next action?
```

Assignment:

```text
Before the next call, every member lists all open change orders and puts a dollar value on unapproved exposure.
```

Retention close:

```text
This is why Contractor Circle exists. Most contractors do not need another motivational call. They need a weekly rhythm that turns hidden leaks into visible money.
```

## Example Call: Project Profit Control

Opening frame:

```text
Today we are fixing the job that looks fine but is quietly losing money.
```

Principle:

```text
You cannot manage project profit through status updates. You need dollars, risk, change orders, and action ownership.
```

Tool:

- Job Profit Snapshot.

Member teardown questions:

```text
Original contract value?
Original gross profit target?
Current cost exposure?
Approved changes?
Pending changes?
Known risks?
Projected margin today?
Top 3 actions this week?
```

Assignment:

```text
Pick one live job and complete a Job Profit Snapshot before the next call.
```

## Example Call: Owner Bottleneck

Opening frame:

```text
Today we are fixing the company that still runs through the owner's head.
```

Principle:

```text
Your people cannot own outcomes until you define the outcomes, numbers, meeting rhythm, and issue-solving process.
```

Tool:

- 10-Hat Owner Scorecard.

Member teardown questions:

```text
Which hat are you wearing that someone else should own?
What decision keeps coming back to you?
What number has no owner?
What issue repeats every week?
What meeting should exist but does not?
```

Assignment:

```text
Choose one hat to begin transferring and define the owner, number, meeting rhythm, and next action.
```

## Immediate Reset Call

Next call title:

```text
The Contractor Profit Leak Reset:
Where Your Company Is Losing Money and What We Are Installing First
```

Agenda:

```text
1. The new direction of Contractor Circle
2. The five profit leaks
3. Why each member needs to know their biggest leak
4. Walkthrough of the Profit Leak Audit
5. Assign each member to a path
6. Show the first tool: Change Order Cash Tracker
7. Explain the 30-day reset
8. Ask every member to post their biggest leak and next action
```

Talking points:

```text
This is not becoming more complicated. It is becoming more organized.

The goal is not to give you more information. The goal is to help you install the systems that keep more money in the company.

Some of you need estimating help. Some of you need change-order control. Some of you need job profit visibility. Some of you are still the operating system. Contractor Circle is where we identify the leak and install the right system.
```

Member assignment:

```text
1. Take the Profit Leak Audit.
2. Post your biggest leak in the Circle.
3. Pick one live job or one current problem.
4. Use the assigned tool.
5. Bring the result to the next call.
```
