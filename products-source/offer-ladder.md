# Contractor Money Systems Offer Ladder

## Strategic Rule

Do not build a course business.

Do not build a coaching business.

Do not build a pile of low-ticket products.

Build one scalable operating ecosystem:

```text
Public media -> Contractor Profit Leak Audit -> nurture -> low-ticket buyer filter -> Contractor Circle -> proprietary tools -> team expansion -> bounded premium access.
```

## One Economic Spine

Every public asset creates pain awareness.

Every pain-awareness path leads to Contractor Circle.

Contractor Circle gives access to the proprietary tools.

The tools create retention.

The calls create implementation, proof, and product research.

The brand creates demand.

## Public Layer

Public promise:

```text
I help contractors stop leaking profit and build companies that do not depend on the owner carrying every decision.
```

Public category:

```text
Contractor Money Systems
```

Deeper doctrine:

```text
The Contractor Operating System
```

Public content hammers five problems:

1. You are winning work and still broke.
2. You are doing extra work for free.
3. Your PMs manage status, not profit.
4. Your company depends on you for every decision.
5. Your people do not own outcomes because you never installed ownership.

## Lead Magnet

### Contractor Profit Leak Audit

Promise:

```text
Find where your company is giving away money before the job, during the job, and after the job.
```

Purpose:

- Capture the broad attention market.
- Segment leads by pain.
- Route every path toward Contractor Circle.

Leak paths:

| Leak type | Their pain | Bridge |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Estimating leak | Winning bad jobs | Estimating system |
| Change-order leak | Free work | Change-order cash process |
| Project profit leak | Jobs look fine until they do not | IOR / OverWatch |
| Owner-dependence leak | Owner is the bottleneck | AOS |
| Accountability leak | People do not own outcomes | Scorecards / roles / rhythms |

### Supporting Lead Magnets

Supporting lead magnets can exist, but they are subordinate to the audit and Circle path.

They are not separate businesses.

Build order:

1. Contractor Profit Leak Audit.
2. Change Order Cash Tracker.
3. Estimating Profit Checklist.
4. 10-Hat Owner Scorecard.

Purpose:

- Capture specific pain.
- Give a concrete quick win.
- Route back to the audit.
- Route forward to Contractor Circle.

## Expansion Tiers

The $497/month tier is the entry point, not the ceiling.

| Tier | Price | Buyer | Included |
| --- | ---: | --- | --- |
| Contractor Circle Core | $497/mo | Owner-led contractor | Owner access, core tools, calls, sprints |
| Contractor Circle Team | $997/mo | Owner + managers | Team seats, OverWatch workflows, PM/accountability tools |
| Contractor Circle Scale | $1,997-$2,997/mo | Larger small business | More seats, quarterly reviews, implementation pathways |
| Contractor Boardroom | $50k-$100k/year | Larger outliers | Bounded strategic access, no unlimited consulting |

## ARR Math

At $497/month:

```text
168 members = roughly $1M ARR
500 members = roughly $2.98M ARR
2,000 members = roughly $11.93M ARR
16,767 members = roughly $100M ARR
```

This means the $497 tier can build a strong business, but the path to tens of millions needs:

- Team tiers.
- Higher ARPU.
- Lower churn through tool adoption.
- Certification / implementation capacity.
- Bounded premium access.

## Proprietary Tool Strategy

Keep the tools proprietary.

Do not make them invisible.

Public should see:

- Screenshots with blurred data.
- Loom walkthroughs.
- Sample company dashboards.
- Teardowns.
- Before/after contractor examples.
- Clips from calls.
- "Here is what members see inside Contractor Circle" posts.

Public should not get the engine.

Message:

```text
You can duct-tape this together yourself, or you can get inside Contractor Circle and use the tools we are already building from real contractor problems every week.
```

## Premium Access

No open-ended one-on-one.

Premium access exists only when it supports the ecosystem.

Examples:

- 90-Day Contractor Operating Intensive: $25k-$50k.
- Annual Contractor Boardroom: $75k-$150k/year.
- Private Implementation License: $100k+/year.

Rules:

- Fixed scope.
- Fixed deliverables.
- Limited access.
- Contractor Circle / app subscription stays active.
- Premium work must produce proof, case studies, product learning, or R&D.

## What Not To Build Next

Do not add more front doors.

Do not add more low-ticket products.

Do not make AOS, IOR, or OverWatch the cold public hook.

Do not let premium advisory become open-ended access.

Do not let calls become low-leverage service delivery.

The next build priority is clarity:

1. One Profit Leak Audit.
2. One Contractor Circle landing page.
3. One master nurture sequence.
4. Two buyer filters.
5. One onboarding sprint.
6. One internal dashboard for leads, conversion, MRR, churn, activation, and tool usage.
