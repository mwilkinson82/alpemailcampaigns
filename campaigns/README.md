# Contractor Money Systems Campaign Machine

This folder turns the Contractor Operating System doctrine into one public acquisition machine.

The machine is not:

- A coaching funnel.
- A course ladder.
- A pile of disconnected low-ticket products.
- A SaaS pitch before the market understands the pain.

The machine is:

```text
Media creates pain awareness.
The Contractor Profit Leak Audit captures and segments the lead.
The nurture sequence educates the leak.
Contractor Circle becomes the obvious implementation environment.
AOS, IOR, OverWatch, Circle Hub, calculators, dashboards, and trackers create retention.
Premium access stays bounded and expensive.
```

## Public Brand Promise

```text
I help contractors stop leaking profit and build companies that do not depend on the owner carrying every decision.
```

## Category Name

Use this publicly:

```text
Contractor Money Systems
```

Use this as the deeper doctrine:

```text
The Contractor Operating System
```

Contractor Money Systems is the better public phrase because it sounds practical. The Contractor Operating System can sit underneath it as the category architecture.

## Market Reality

The larger, sophisticated contractors are outliers.

The rule is owner-led, under-systemized, cash-leaky, trade-heavy, small construction businesses.

The smallest contractors are a large attention market. The best subscription buyers are growth-stage owner-operators who have moved beyond pure survival and now feel operational pain.

Best ICP:

```text
Owner-led contractors doing roughly $1M-$15M+ in revenue with crews, PMs, estimators, subs, change orders, cash-flow problems, and an owner who is still the hub of everything.
```

The public audience can be broader. The paid subscription machine should qualify toward the ICP.

## The Five Public Problems

Public content should hammer these five problems:

1. You are winning work and still broke.
2. You are doing extra work for free.
3. Your PMs manage status, not profit.
4. Your company depends on you for every decision.
5. Your people do not own outcomes because you never installed ownership.

Do not lead with AOS, IOR, OverWatch, accountability, roles, responsibilities, or operating-system language.

Those are mechanisms.

The public hook is money and control.

## Primary Lead Magnet

There is one primary lead magnet:

```text
Contractor Profit Leak Audit
```

Promise:

```text
Find where your company is giving away money before the job, during the job, and after the job.
```

The audit segments leads into five leak types:

| Leak type | Their pain | Your bridge |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Estimating leak | Winning bad jobs | Estimating system |
| Change-order leak | Free work | Change-order cash process |
| Project profit leak | Jobs look fine until they do not | IOR / OverWatch |
| Owner-dependence leak | Owner is the bottleneck | AOS |
| Accountability leak | People do not own outcomes | Scorecards / roles / rhythms |

## Low-Ticket Filters

Low-ticket products are not the business.

They are buyer-intent filters.

Keep only two active filters at first:

1. **Change Order Cash Kit**
   - Clearest pain.
   - Public promise: `Stop doing extra work for free.`

2. **Project Profit Control Sprint**
   - Best bridge into IOR / OverWatch.
   - Public promise: `Manage one live job through dollars, risk, and profit.`

The rule:

```text
The free audit shows the leak.
The kit helps patch one leak.
Contractor Circle fixes the company.
```

## Supporting Lead Magnets

Supporting lead magnets can exist, but they are not separate businesses.

They are subordinate capture tools:

1. **Change Order Cash Tracker**
   - Fastest money pain.
   - Routes to the Profit Leak Audit and Contractor Circle.

2. **Estimating Profit Checklist**
   - Captures contractors who want more or better work.
   - Routes to the Profit Leak Audit and Contractor Circle.

3. **10-Hat Owner Scorecard**
   - Bridges into AOS without corporate language.
   - Routes to the Profit Leak Audit and Contractor Circle.

## Proprietary Tool Rule

Keep the tools proprietary, but not invisible.

Public should see:

- Screenshots with blurred data.
- Loom walkthroughs.
- Sample company dashboards.
- Teardown videos.
- Before/after stories.
- Clips from calls.
- "Here is what members see inside Contractor Circle" content.

Public should not get the engine.

## Premium Access Rule

No open-ended one-on-one.

Premium access must be bounded, expensive, and supportive of the ecosystem.

It should fund R&D, produce proof, create case studies, and validate the system.

It should not become the business.
